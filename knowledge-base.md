## Google Ads Master Guide — Added 2026-03-25

### Why Google Ads Works For Roofing Calls
- Roofing is one of the highest-intent verticals on Google — people searching "roof repair near me" have their wallet out
- Average CPC: $10-$30/click, up to $40-$60 in competitive storm markets
- Phone calls convert to jobs 10-15x more than form fills — always optimize for calls
- Google needs 15-30 conversions/month per campaign to optimize — keep campaigns consolidated
- New account: start Maximize Clicks to gather data, switch to Maximize Conversions after 15+ conversions
- Quality Score determines ad position — lower bid + higher QS can outrank higher bidder

### Keyword Intent Tiers

**Tier 1 — Emergency (BEST for pay-per-call):**
- "roof leak repair near me" | "emergency roof repair [city]" | "roof damage after storm" | "roof leaking right now"
- CPC: $15-$40 | Intent: BUYING NOW

**Tier 2 — Service Specific (HIGH value):**
- "roof replacement [city]" | "storm damage roof repair" | "hail damage roof assessment" | "roofing contractor near me"
- CPC: $10-$25 | Intent: Ready to hire

**Tier 3 — Research Phase (AVOID for calls):**
- "how much does roof repair cost" | "best roofing materials" — lower call conversion, don't waste budget here

### Keyword Research Tools
1. Google Keyword Planner (FREE) — Tools → Keyword Planner → Discover new keywords. Shows search volume + top of page bids
2. Google Search Terms Report — After running ads, shows exactly what triggered your ads. Mine weekly for negatives
3. Google Trends — FREE, shows storm season spikes by state. Use for weather-triggered bid increases

### Match Types Strategy — 2026

**Exact Match [keyword]** — tightest control, most expensive per click, use for proven converters
**Phrase Match "keyword"** — START HERE for new campaigns. Steady volume, reasonable relevance
**Broad Match keyword** — AVOID until 30+ conversions/month. Dangerous with $15-30 CPCs without strong negative list

**Progression:**
- Phase 1 (0-30 days): Phrase match only. Build negatives weekly from search terms report
- Phase 2 (30-60 days): Promote consistent converters to exact match
- Phase 3 (60+ days, 30+ conversions/month): Add broad match + Target CPA Smart Bidding

### Master Negative Keyword List (Add Before Launch)

**Job/Career searches:**
jobs, hiring, career, salary, employment, apprentice, training, courses, certification

**DIY searches:**
DIY, how to, myself, yourself, tutorial, guide, materials only, supplies, tools

**Wrong intent:**
free, cheap, cheapest, reviews, complaints, scam, vs, comparison

**Wrong services (if roofing only):**
solar, gutters, siding, painting, windows, commercial (if residential only)

**Add as exact match negatives first, expand to phrase match after seeing patterns in search terms report**

### Campaign Structure For RCN (Keep Simple)

**Campaign 1 — Emergency/Storm (60% of budget):**
Keywords: "emergency roof repair", "roof leak repair", "storm damage roof", "hail damage roof"
Ad copy: Urgency — "Roof Leaking Now? Call Within Minutes"

**Campaign 2 — General Roofing (40% of budget):**
Keywords: "roof repair near me", "roofing contractor", "roof replacement estimate"
Ad copy: Trust — "Licensed Contractors Nationwide · (866) 466-9261"

**NEVER split into more than 2 campaigns at start — algorithm needs 15-30 conversions per campaign per month**

### Bid Strategy Progression

1. **New account (0-30 days):** Maximize Clicks + max CPC cap of $15-20
2. **After 15+ conversions:** Maximize Conversions — don't touch for 2 weeks
3. **After 30+ conversions/month:** Target CPA — set at 60-70% of dynamic payout for margin
4. **Never increase budget more than 20% at a time** — resets learning phase

### Quality Score Improvement

QS = Expected CTR (40%) + Ad Relevance (30%) + Landing Page Experience (30%)

- Include target keyword in Headline 1 of every ad
- Use call extensions — shows (866) 466-9261 directly in the ad
- Ad copy must match landing page message exactly
- Landing page loads under 3 seconds on mobile
- One clear CTA — phone number, big, above fold

### Ad Copy Formulas That Work

**Headlines:**
- "Storm Damage Roof Repair · Call Now"
- "Roof Leaking After The Storm?"
- "Licensed Contractors · Same Day Response"
- "4.9 Stars · 14,800+ Homeowners Helped"
- "Serving All 50 States · Call (866) 466-9261"

**Descriptions:**
- "Don't let storm damage get worse. Get a professional assessment from a licensed contractor before costs escalate."
- "Licensed, bonded & insured contractors. Fast response, transparent pricing, no obligation to proceed."

**Compliance: NEVER use free, free inspection, $0 cost, or insurance references in any ad copy**

### Call Tracking Setup (Critical)
- Google Ads → Goals → Conversions → New Conversion → Phone Calls from ads
- Set minimum call duration to match your dynamic buffer
- Without call tracking you cannot optimize — set this up before spending a dollar

### Google Ads Benchmarks (2026)
- Average roofing CPC: $10.70 nationwide, up to $40 in storm corridors
- Average roofing CPL via Google Search: $228 — highest of all home services
- Good click-to-call conversion rate: 10-15%
- Strong conversion rate: 20%+ (great landing page + message match)
- Smart Bidding with broad match = 25-35% more conversions (needs conversion data first)
- Without negatives: 20-40% of budget goes to job searches and DIY queries

### Storm Season Bid Strategy
- Pre-storm: Increase bids 20-30% in states with active weather
- Post-storm first 72 hours: HIGHEST intent window, emergency searches spike 40%
- Top storm states for bid increases: TX, OK, KS, NE, MO, IA, IL, OH, CO, MN
- Hurricane states (June-November): FL, LA, SC, NC

### Common Google Ads Mistakes To Avoid
1. Too many campaigns — algorithm never learns. MAX 2 to start
2. No negative keywords — 20-40% of budget wasted on irrelevant searches
3. Homepage as landing page — always use service-specific pages
4. Starting broad match with no conversion data — budget bleeds fast
5. No call extensions — you MUST show the phone number in the ad
6. Making changes more than once per week — let campaigns learn
7. Judging performance in the first 2 weeks — Smart Bidding needs time
